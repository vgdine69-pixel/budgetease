üìö How to Calculate Your Budget

Step 1: Calculate Total Income

Add up all sources of monthly income including salary, freelance work, investments, and other income. Use after-tax income (take-home pay) for accurate budget calculation.

Step 2: List All Expenses

Write down all monthly expenses including rent, utilities, groceries, transportation, insurance, debt payments, entertainment, and miscellaneous expenses. BudgetEase budget calculator helps you categorize everything.

Step 3: Apply the 50/30/20 Rule

The budget calculator uses the proven 50/30/20 rule: 50% for needs (housing, food, utilities), 30% for wants (entertainment, dining out), and 20% for savings and debt repayment.

Step 4: Calculate Category Budgets

Use the budget calculator to determine exact amounts for each spending category based on your income and the 50/30/20 allocation.

Step 5: Adjust for Your Situation

Customize the calculated budget based on your specific needs. If you have high debt, allocate more to debt repayment. If saving for a house, increase savings percentage.

Step 6: Track Actual vs Calculated

Monitor your actual spending against the calculated budget. BudgetEase shows real-time comparison and alerts you when exceeding budget limits.

Step 7: Recalculate Monthly

Recalculate your budget monthly or when income changes. The budget calculator makes it easy to adjust and optimize your budget over time.
